# Research Quality Appraisal

## Core Rule

Use the appraisal tool that matches the study design. Do not produce a generic
quality score unless the selected tool explicitly requires one. Explain judgments
with evidence from the paper.

## Tool Selection

- Randomized trials: Cochrane RoB 2.
- Non-randomized intervention studies: ROBINS-I.
- Systematic reviews: AMSTAR 2.
- Observational cohort/case-control/cross-sectional studies: NIH, JBI, or CASP
  design-specific tools.
- Diagnostic accuracy: QUADAS-2.
- Qualitative studies: CASP qualitative or JBI qualitative checklist.
- Mixed-methods studies: MMAT.
- Overall certainty across a body of evidence: GRADE.

## Appraisal Workflow

1. Classify the study.
   - Identify design, assignment mechanism, comparator, time ordering, unit of
     analysis, outcome type, and whether claims are causal, predictive,
     descriptive, diagnostic, or qualitative.

2. Select the appraisal framework.
   - State the selected tool and why it fits.
   - If the paper combines designs, appraise each evidence component separately.

3. Judge key domains.
   - Selection/recruitment and baseline comparability.
   - Randomization or confounding control.
   - Intervention/exposure measurement.
   - Outcome measurement validity and blinding.
   - Missing data and attrition.
   - Selective reporting and protocol consistency.
   - Analysis appropriateness, multiplicity, clustering, and model assumptions.
   - Applicability to the target population or setting.

4. Provide transparent judgments.
   - Use categories from the chosen tool when possible.
   - Quote or paraphrase the paper's method/result details that justify each
     judgment.
   - Mark unclear when information is absent; do not infer adequate methods from
     polished prose.

5. Grade body-level certainty when asked.
   - Start from study design and adjust for risk of bias, inconsistency,
     indirectness, imprecision, and publication bias.
   - Upgrade observational evidence only when GRADE conditions are clearly met.

## Output Pattern

For each study:

- Study design and claim type.
- Appraisal tool selected.
- Domain judgments with rationale.
- Major threats to validity.
- Applicability/generalizability notes.
- Overall risk-of-bias or quality judgment.
- What conclusions this study can and cannot support.

For a body of evidence:

- Outcome-specific certainty.
- GRADE-style downgrade/upgrade reasons.
- Confidence statement in plain language.

## Guardrails

- Do not equate journal prestige, citation count, or sample size with quality.
- Do not collapse risk of bias, reporting quality, and relevance into one label.
- Do not use randomized-trial criteria to penalize qualitative studies.
- Do not call evidence "high quality" when key methods are unreported.
- If full text is unavailable, label appraisal as title/abstract-only and
  provisional.
