# Literature Review Synthesis

## Core Rule

Synthesize evidence, not abstracts. Separate what the studies found, how they
were designed, how trustworthy they are, and what claim the evidence can support.

## Workflow

1. Identify review type.
   - Systematic review: pre-specified question, exhaustive search, reproducible
     screening, quality appraisal, and transparent synthesis.
   - Scoping review: maps concepts, evidence types, and gaps; avoid effect-size
     conclusions unless the underlying studies support them.
   - Narrative review: can be selective, but must state selection logic and avoid
     presenting convenience samples as comprehensive.
   - Rapid review: state shortcuts and likely bias introduced by shortcuts.

2. Build an extraction matrix.
   - Capture citation, DOI/URL, country/setting, design, sample, population,
     intervention/exposure, comparator, outcomes, follow-up, measures, main
     results, limitations, funding/conflicts, and notes for quality appraisal.
   - For qualitative evidence, capture phenomenon, data source, analytic method,
     participant context, themes, and reflexivity notes.
   - Mark missing information explicitly; do not fill gaps from assumptions.

3. Group studies before writing.
   - Group by question, population, intervention/exposure, outcome, setting,
     design, or theory depending on the review purpose.
   - Keep randomized, observational, qualitative, and modeling studies separate
     until their evidence roles are clear.
   - Distinguish direct evidence from adjacent or mechanistic evidence.

4. Synthesize cautiously.
   - State direction, consistency, magnitude, precision, and applicability.
   - Explain heterogeneity instead of flattening it.
   - Use narrative synthesis when meta-analysis is inappropriate.
   - Never claim causality from cross-sectional or uncontrolled evidence.
   - Separate confirmatory findings from exploratory, pilot, feasibility, and
     hypothesis-generating findings.

5. Produce review-ready prose.
   - Start each paragraph with the evidence question, not the paper title.
   - Use studies as evidence for a claim, then immediately qualify by design,
     bias, sample, or generalizability.
   - End sections with a decision-relevant implication or gap.

## Output Pattern

For an evidence table:

- Citation/DOI.
- Design/sample/setting.
- Intervention/exposure/comparator.
- Outcomes/measures.
- Main findings.
- Limitations and appraisal notes.
- Role in synthesis.

For prose synthesis:

- Claim.
- Evidence base supporting the claim.
- Heterogeneity or conflicting evidence.
- Quality/certainty caveat.
- Practical or theoretical implication.

## Guardrails

- Do not overclaim from small pilots, single-arm studies, uncontrolled before-after
  studies, or convenience samples.
- Do not merge different outcomes into one conclusion without explaining the link.
- Do not treat statistical significance as clinical, educational, or practical
  importance.
- If quality appraisal has not been done, label certainty as provisional and
  invoke the research-quality-appraisal skill.

## Standards To Anchor

Use PRISMA 2020 for systematic review reporting, PRISMA-ScR for scoping reviews,
SWiM for synthesis without meta-analysis, Cochrane Handbook synthesis guidance,
and GRADE when moving from study findings to certainty of evidence.
